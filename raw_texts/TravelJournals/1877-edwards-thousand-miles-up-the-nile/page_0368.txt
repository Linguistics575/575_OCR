In order to have done with this subject, it may be as well to
mention that another trilingual tablet was found by M. Mariette while
conducting the excavations at 85.11 (Tanis) in 1865. It dates from the
ninth year of Ptolemy Evergetes, and the text ordains the deiﬁcation
of Berenice, a daughter of the king, then just dead (B.C. 254). This
stone, preserved in the museum at Boulak, is known as the Stone of
Sfm, or the Decree of Canopus. Had the Rosetta Stone never been
discovered, we may fairly conclude that the CanOpic Decree would
have furnished some later Champollion with the necessary key to hiero-
glyphic literature, and that the great discovery would only have been
deferred till the present time.

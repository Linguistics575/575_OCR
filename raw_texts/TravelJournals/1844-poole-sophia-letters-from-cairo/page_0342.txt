On each side of the Great Pyramid is an accu-
mulation of fragments of stone and mortar which
have fallen down from the summit and sides of the
building, and have composed a very compact mass,
which rises, in the centre, to about ﬁfty feet above
the base. The sand of the desert has contributed
but little to augment these slopes of rubbish, which
are nearly of the same height on each side of the
pyramid. That on the northern side forms a con-
venient acclivity to the entrance.
